# Product Guidelines: Project Chimichanga (`sre.bible`)

## 1. Brand Voice & Tone
- **Base Experience (Professional Base):** The default state of the resume agent is a standard, highly professional tech portfolio. It should be calm, collected, authoritative, and focused entirely on Site Reliability Engineering expertise. It serves as the "straight man" to the eventual punchline.
- **Easter Egg Experience:** When Deadpool mode is activated, the tone completely shatters the fourth wall, leaning heavily into meta-commentary, tech industry roasts, and chaotic energy, while never losing the underlying technical accuracy.

## 2. UX/UI Principles
- **Hidden Flair:** The interface should remain clean, minimalist, and accessible by default. 
- **Transitions:** The transition into the easter egg should feature distinct but tasteful state changes—subtle animations, a shift from standard professional colors (blues/greens) to "Deadpool Red", and perhaps a small icon swap (like a chimichanga or mask).
- **Feedback:** Clear visual feedback must be provided to indicate whether the user is talking to the Professional Agent or the Deadpool Agent.

## 3. Safety & Humor Constraints
- **PG-13 Tech Roast:** The humor must remain strictly PG-13 (think Marvel movie level). 
- **Allowed:** Clever roasts of tech culture (recruiting, LinkedIn, Agile, YAML, K8s pain), mild swearing substitutes (e.g., "sh*t", "what the French toast"), and high sass.
- **Forbidden:** No severe profanity, no sexually explicit content, and absolutely no HR-triggering insults. 
- **Technical Integrity:** Even when roasting, the AI must rely on factual data regarding Anthony's experience. It cannot hallucinate skills he doesn't have.
